Party game / communication challenge

Chrono Explain

One player is from 2026. Everyone else is from another era. Explain a modern thing in two minutes without leaning on modern assumptions, technical jargon, or obvious concepts your audience has never seen.

How to play

  1. Pick Player 1, the modern explainer.
  2. Generate a modern object and a historical audience.
  3. Player 1 has 2 minutes to explain it.
  4. Judges decide whether the audience understood what it is, what it does, and why people use it.

Player 1 goal

Explain the thing well enough that the audience can describe it back in their own words.

Avoid saying

  • Technical terms
  • It is obvious
  • Modern brand names

Era-friendly angle

Use metaphors from the audience's world, not yours.

Judge the explanation

A successful round needs at least 4 of 6 points. For a harder game, require 5.

Explanation seed

Round history

Variant: historical players can ask questions only from their own worldview. A person from 400 BC can ask about gods, animals, fire, craft, trade, war, ships, scribes, and rulers — not apps, batteries, plastics, satellites, or factories.
